Group Project Card Game

Spell Forge

Timeframe: 5 Weeks
Role: Card and Game Designer
Tools: Tabletop Simulator
Skills: Teamwork, Communication

A card game best described as a mix between blackjack and poker. Created for tabletop simulator, Spell Forge is a game where you try to build the most powerful "spell" in your hand while also keeping it within the complexity limit that was set for that round. Even further, additional power is added to the spell if you manage to get flushes and/or straights between the cards in the spell. Every card has its own complexity, power, and type which allows for endless spell combinations, but only 1 spell can win each round. Spell Forge was a group project for which I created the card designs and helped balance all the cards' stats and the game's rules.

The game is set in the Ethshar universe and heavily uses its idea of various components that make up each spell in wizardry. Almost all of the cards in the game are from a list of common spell components but the art for each of the components was drawn by other members of the team.

The game's balance has changed drastically over the course of its development. At first, high complexity cards were given high power amounts and were supposed to be the best cards to use if you could afford their complexity. But, it turned out that it was better to keep a lot of low complexity cards instead as they offered more power in the long run. So the complexity and power amounts on the cards were tweaked so that the higher complexity cards had reduced complexity and higher power. This change made the lower complexity cards a lot weaker but worked well for the higher complexity cards. Finally, to balance both sides out we added card matches that would award bonus points for getting any flushes or straights. Doing this was a lot easier with more cards and so for this strategy, lower complexity cards are more favorable. So with all of these changes all the cards have their proper uses and feel more balanced than in the beginning.

    Challenges:
  • Displaying all of the most important information of each card in the easiest to see places on the card.
  • Describing the rules of the game in the simplest way possible for new players.
  • Balancing the cards so that they all have their appropriate uses.
  • Making the game less reliant on mental math.

    What I did well:
  • Cards have a consistent, pleasing design that fits the narrative.
  • The same card can be used in many different ways depending on the situation.
  • Games normally only take an hour or less, and players use their own creativity to win and outsmart their opponents.

    What I would change/improve:
  • The game still relies on the player's mental math for them to understand their current hand. Scaling the numbers on each card down while maintaining their balance would allow for quicker math. Requiring more players to play would also give each player more time to think about what to keep and discard.
  • Develop new terms for flush and straight that make the concept easier to understand.
