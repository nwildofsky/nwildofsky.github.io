Solo Project Custom Scene 3D Modeling

Treasure Room Scene

Timeframe: 2 Months
Role: 3D Modeler and Level Designer
Tools: Maya, Surface Painter, Photoshop, Unity
Skills: 3D Modeling, Texturing, and Lighting

A showcase of my 3D modeling, UV setting, and texturing skills as well as an exercise of my knowledge of Unity and its lighting. I created all the 3D models and their UV sets with Autodesk Maya and textured them with Adobe Substance Painter. I had been using Maya for 3D modeling for a few months at this point but was completely new to Substance Painter and learned it through some tutorial videos and my own experimentation. This project also allowed me to try using Unity VFX Graph for the first time, which I used to create the fire effects in the scene. The scene itself is a fantasy-themed dungeon tresure room that a player might find through exploring if it were in a larger game.

    Challenges:
  • Modeling and texturing each object for the scene at a fast pace while also keeping a consistent level of quality.
  • Modeling complex shapes and curves while also guaranteeing the geometry had no errors when setting the UVs.
  • Learning how to use Adobe Substance Painter to texture all my models.
  • Lighting the scene how I wanted it when each major change to the lighting entailed a long baking process that would take hours.

    What I did well:
  • Chose the create models that fit well with the theme and were easy to duplicate and fill up the scene in a natural way.
  • Lead the player's eyes from the entrance to the most important aspect of the scene, the main treasure chest.
  • Created different textures for repeated models, like the books, for variation.
  • Positioned each of the objects in a believable way so they don't look "placed" into the scene.

    What I would change/improve:
  • I knew what kind of effect I wanted from the sky light entering the scene from the back, the rays of light illuminating the backside of the treasure, but I should have done it with windows rather than a strange opening in the wall.
  • Make use of the large number of smaller objects placed in the scene and add collision physics to the player and these items so they'd be able to knock them around.
